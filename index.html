PhD Research

Towards a Digital Twin for Water Resource Management

Institution: IRISA (Research Institute of Computer Science and Random Systems), Rennes

Start Date: May 1st, 2025

Context: Part of the IRIS-E ANIME-WATER multidisciplinary project (Computer Science, Hydrogeophysics, Hydrology, Sociology).

Description: Addressing the critical challenge of water resource management exacerbated by climate change and population growth, this research *will* focus on designing and developing an advanced Digital Twin of a water production system. The goal is to create an innovative tool capable of integrating diverse simulation models, facilitating co-construction of prospective scenarios with stakeholders, and providing clear visualizations to support informed decision-making.

Key Scientific Challenges & Objectives:
  • Flexible Model Integration: Designing a platform for seamless integration of various scientific models.
  • Multidisciplinary Model Coupling: Enabling the coupling of models representing different perspectives (e.g., hydrology, system dynamics).
  • Scenario Adaptability: Developing methods to select and configure appropriate models based on specific end-user scenarios.
  • Effective Results Visualization: Translating complex simulation outputs into clear, actionable representations (in collaboration with a Sociology PhD student).

This research resides at the intersection of scientific modeling, water management, digital technologies, and human-computer interaction, aiming to significantly advance sustainable water resource management practices.
